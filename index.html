About Me

I was born in York, PA in May of 1985. My family moved to Danvers, MA in the Greater Boston Area in 1992 when my dad got a job as the Assistant Director of Respiratory Therapy at Mass General Hospital. I graduated high school in 2003 and went to Simmons College in Boston, MA to pursue a degree in Neuroscience. I graduated college in 2007, expecting to take a couple years off before appling to grad school, hoping to continue with research in cognitive/behavioral neuroscience.

I was given the opportunity to work in a Sleep Research Lab at Brigham and Women's Hospital in Boston, MA as a research assistant. I was sent to a training program to become a Registerd Polysomnologic Technologist (RPSGT), or "Sleep Tech" and have been working in the field ever since. I started as an entry level research assistant, and will be ending my career in the Sleep Research as a Senior Research Lab Manager.

Working for almost 14 years in a sleep research lab was both fulfilling and challenging, and I am great to have the experiences I did learning every aspect of running a research lab, from grant writing to publication, and everything in between (recruitment, data collection, and data analysis). I was amn intergral part of a team of researchers who are regarded as the best in their field. Regardless of my previous position in a globally respected research lab, I still feel an urge to pursue something new. Which brings me to coding!

I know many people who have changed careers to coding after being in a steady career for many years. Everyone I talk to agrees, coding is lucrartive field that is challenging just as much as it is rewarding. And I can't wait to find out for myself!
